<p><ul><li>Suspension</li><li>Emulsion</li><li>Continuous mass polymerization</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Styrene-Acrylonitrile (SAN) resin market comprises three main types of polymerization methods: suspension, emulsion, and continuous mass polymerization. </p><p>Suspension polymerization allows for the production of SAN in discrete particles, enabling better control over the resin properties. Emulsion polymerization, on the other hand, results in smaller particle sizes and improved processing characteristics. Continuous mass polymerization offers high efficiency and consistency, producing SAN in bulk without the need for solvents. Each method influences the resin's final applications and performance characteristics in various industries.</p></p>
