<p><p>Bedside monitoring systems are categorized into three market types: high-acuity monitors, mid-acuity monitors, and low-acuity monitors. High-acuity monitors are designed for critical care settings, providing advanced metrics for patients with severe conditions. Mid-acuity monitors cater to patients needing intermediate levels of observation, typically used in general wards. Low-acuity monitors are employed for stable patients, focusing on basic vital sign tracking and ensuring safety in outpatient settings. Each type serves distinct clinical environments based on patient needs.</p></p>
