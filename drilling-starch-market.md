<p><p>The drilling starch market is categorized into high-temperature and low-temperature starches, each serving specific needs in oil and gas drilling operations. High-temperature starch is designed to maintain viscosity and stability under extreme heat conditions, suitable for deeper wells. In contrast, low-temperature starch is formulated for cooler environments, providing effective fluid loss control and enhancing wellbore stability. Both types are essential for optimizing drilling performance, ensuring efficient operations while minimizing issues related to temperature variations.</p></p>
