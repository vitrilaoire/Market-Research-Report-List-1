<p><p>Graphene nanosheets are classified into two main market types based on their surface properties: hydrophilic and hydrophobic. Hydrophilic graphene nanosheets have a strong affinity for water, promoting easier dispersion in aqueous solutions and facilitating applications in biomedical and environmental fields. In contrast, hydrophobic graphene nanosheets repel water, enhancing their use in applications requiring high surface area and durability, such as in coatings and electronics. These distinct properties cater to diverse industrial demands and innovation opportunities in graphene technology.</p></p>
