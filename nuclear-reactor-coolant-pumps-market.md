<p><p>The nuclear reactor coolant pumps market is categorized by reactor types: Pressurized Water Reactors (PWR) utilize coolant pumps to circulate water under high pressure, preventing boiling; Boiling Water Reactors (BWR) allow water to boil in the reactor core, using steam to drive turbines. Pressurized Heavy Water Reactors (PHWR) use heavy water as a coolant, while Gas-cooled Reactors circulate gas as a coolant. Light Water Graphite Reactors combine graphite moderation with light water for cooling, whereas Fast Neutron Reactors employ liquid metal or gas to cool, allowing faster fission reactions.</p></p>
