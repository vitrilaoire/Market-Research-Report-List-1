<p><p>The nuclear energy market comprises three main types: nuclear fission, nuclear fusion, and nuclear decay. Nuclear fission involves splitting heavy atomic nuclei to release energy, primarily used in reactors for electricity generation. Nuclear fusion, the process of combining light atomic nuclei, holds potential for cleaner energy but is still in experimental stages. Nuclear decay refers to the natural breakdown of unstable isotopes, leading to the release of energy and radiation, often harnessed in medical applications and isotopic power sources.</p></p>
